Build Process

Follow the journey of creating our LED Resin Cube from concept to completion

The Development Journey

Our LED Resin Cube went through several phases of development, from initial concept sketches to the final product. Below you'll find documentation of each major step in our build process.

Step 1: Initial Design

Initial design sketch of the cube structure

3D modeling of the cube in CAD software

The first step in our process was creating detailed designs for the cube structure. We started with hand sketches to explore different form factors and configurations of LEDs within the resin medium. After several iterations, we settled on a 10cm cube design with a 5x5x5 grid of individually addressable RGB LEDs.

We then moved to 3D modeling software to create precise technical drawings that would guide our physical build. This stage was crucial for determining the exact positioning of electronic components and planning the resin casting process.

Step 2: Electronics Assembly

Preparing the custom PCB for assembly

Arranging the LED matrix configuration

Once our design was finalized, we began the electronics assembly phase. This started with fabricating our custom PCB that would serve as the control center for the LED array. We carefully soldered the microcontroller, power regulators, and IR receiver components to the board.

The most challenging part was creating the 3D LED matrix. Each LED needed to be precisely positioned to create the desired visual effect when illuminated. We used thin gauge wire to connect the LEDs in series, ensuring that each could be individually controlled by the microcontroller.

Step 3: Resin Casting

Preparing the silicone mold for resin casting

Carefully pouring the resin in layers

The resin casting process required careful planning and execution. We created a custom silicone mold to achieve the perfect cube shape. The process involved pouring the resin in multiple layers to properly suspend the LED matrix in the center of the cube.

Each layer needed to cure for 24 hours before the next could be added. We had to be extremely careful not to disturb the LED positioning during this process. After the final pour, the complete cube needed to cure for an additional 72 hours before it could be removed from the mold and finished.

Step 4: Programming and Testing

Developing the control software

Testing LED patterns and IR responsiveness

With the physical cube completed, we moved on to programming the microcontroller to create various lighting patterns and respond to IR input. We developed several animation algorithms that would create visually striking patterns within the 3D LED matrix.

The IR wand controller required its own programming for sending commands to the cube. We implemented different gesture recognition patterns that would trigger specific light animations when detected by the IR receiver.

Final Result

The completed LED Resin Cube

The cube displaying a light pattern

After weeks of development, design iterations, and careful assembly, our LED Resin Cube was finally complete. The finished product features a crystal-clear resin body that perfectly showcases the 3D matrix of LEDs inside.

The cube responds instantly to IR wand gestures, creating dynamic lighting patterns that can be controlled by the user. We're extremely proud of the final result and the technical challenges we overcame during the build process.
